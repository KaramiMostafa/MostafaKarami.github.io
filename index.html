About

I’m a Ph.D. student in Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Connecticut. My work focuses on 3D breast tomosynthesis and microscopic images, with methods for cell tracking, feature-based registration, and robust machine learning under distribution shift.

Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant — University of Connecticut

Storrs, CT, USA · 2023 – Present

  • Designing a Bayesian Transformer and higher-order graph matching pipeline for tracking cells in serial tissue sections, enabling more accurate 3D reconstructions for ultraplex imaging.
  • Developing CAPTURE and ImageReg — feature-based registration frameworks that combine SIFT/SURF-style keypoints with ResNet50 embeddings for distortion analysis and robust alignment of large-scale biological images.
  • Building registration and tracking codebases with reproducible evaluation pipelines, supporting uncertainty estimation and integration into downstream quantitative analyses.

Research Assistant — University of Cologne

Cologne, Germany · 2022 – 2023

  • Applied machine learning to radiomic MRI features to predict MGMT promoter methylation in glioblastoma patients, leveraging 1153 features extracted with Pyradiomics (including LoG and wavelet-filtered descriptors).
  • Used XGBoost for feature selection and trained LR, SVM, and MLP classifiers with nested cross-validation across FLAIR, T1w, T1Gd, and T2 sequences.
  • Managed data processing, documentation, and GitHub repositories to ensure reproducible end-to-end pipelines for radiogenomics studies.

Graduate Research Assistant — Politecnico di Torino

Turin, Italy · 2021 – 2022

  • Modeled the relationship between air-pollution exposure (PM measures) and CNS disease mortality (Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s) using regression and time-series forecasting.
  • Processed large-scale environmental and epidemiological datasets, building data-driven models to quantify risk at regional scales.
  • Implemented interactive dashboards to visualize spatiotemporal trends and communicate findings to clinical and public-health collaborators.
