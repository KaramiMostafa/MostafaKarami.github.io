About

I’m a Ph.D. student in Computer Science & Engineering at the University of Connecticut. My work focuses on 3D digital breast tomosynthesis and microscopic images, with methods for cell tracking, feature-based registration, and robust ML for noisy, distribution-shifted biomedical data.

Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant — University of Connecticut

Storrs, CT, USA · 2023–Present

  • Designing a Bayesian Transformer and higher-order graph matching pipeline for cell tracking in serial tissue sections, enabling accurate 3D reconstructions for ultraplex imaging.
  • Developing CAPTURE and ImageReg: feature-based registration frameworks that combine SIFT/SURF-style keypoints with ResNet50 embeddings for distortion analysis and robust alignment of large biological images.
  • Building reproducible registration and tracking codebases with uncertainty estimation and evaluation pipelines for large-scale imaging studies.

Research Assistant — University of Cologne

Cologne, Germany · 2022–2023

  • Applied machine learning to MR-based radiomic features to predict MGMT promoter methylation in glioblastoma.
  • Extracted and processed 1153 features using Pyradiomics (including LoG and wavelet filters) and used XGBoost for feature selection with LR, SVM, and MLP models trained via nested cross-validation.
  • Managed data curation, preprocessing pipelines, and GitHub-hosted code to ensure reproducible radiogenomics analyses.

Graduate Research Assistant — Politecnico di Torino

Turin, Italy · 2021–2022

  • Analyzed air pollution particles in young brains to investigate links to neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s.
  • Developed data-driven ML models relating particulate matter exposure to CNS disease mortality and built regression/forecasting models to estimate future risk.
  • Created interactive dashboards for exploring spatial and temporal patterns, supporting collaboration between engineers, clinicians, and public-health researchers.
